FonsClarus Engineering is a technical blog operated alongside a dedicated YouTube channel. Its purpose is the factual, in-depth dissemination of knowledge in the field of embedded systems — with a focus on microcontrollers by STMicroelectronics (STM32) and the ARM Cortex-M architecture.

## Coverage

- **STM32 & ARM Cortex-M**: Register-level programming, HAL/CMSIS, start-up code, linker scripts, peripheral drivers
- **Firmware Engineering**: Bare-metal implementations, state machines, communication protocols (SPI, I²C, UART, CAN), debugging with OpenOCD/GDB
- **Toolchain & Workflow**: GCC/Binutils, CMake, Docker, CI/CD for embedded projects, version control
- **Measurement & Verification**: Oscilloscope measurements, logic analysis, performance analysis (clock cycles, latencies)

The articles are aimed at developers who seek not merely working code but a thorough understanding of the underlying hardware and the ARM Cortex-M architecture. All results — unless stated otherwise — are based on original measurements and reproducible test setups.

## About the YouTube Channel

This blog serves as a companion platform to the YouTube channel. While the channel presents concepts visually and in chronological workflow, the blog allows topics to be elaborated in written form with precision, supplemented by source code, tables, and measurement data, and made permanently referenceable.
